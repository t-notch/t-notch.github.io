About

As an engineer, I love to explore, tweak and build things and sometimes break things just to satisfy my curiosity and hunger for creativity.

As a father of 2 boys, exploring, hacking and building stuffs is best way to have fun and learn new things together.

Exploring and learning are human nature! Observing how the world works, using the good things and changing the bad things for our own good is how we as human race develop through generations. Through out history of human being, everyday we faced challenges from the world, and from ourselves. Loosing jobs because of new technologies, extremist threats, nature disasters, diseases … are not something we only face now, our ancestors faced them in the past, and our children will face them in future. However, the past passed, the future is to come. “Will they success?”, it is the question for the education we give them now. Not only to learn about nature, about new technologies, about how the world works, but also what is good, what is bad and how to apply and use them for the sake of our race, our planet.

To do this, hacking is the way, and it is the fun way: to explore the limits of what is possible, thereby doing something exciting and meaningful. That hacking is purely activities of playful cleverness! However, from knowing the world and changing it is not always joyful journey, there will be failures, mislucks, … but that is normal, that is when we know the world better. Keypoint is we must adopt a hacking mindset.

Hacking is a mindset, not a skillset. When you seek, in your everyday life, to deliberately find opportunities to be clever, ethical, to enjoy what you are doing, to seek excellence, then you’re hacking.

These thoughts are from Tanya Snook (@Spydergrrl) writing in an article for LSE’s blog. As a tech-nerd, I adopted and lived that mindset. As a dad, I wouldn’t agree more:

It’s important to teach kids to hack and actually call it that; to explain to them the importance of reinventing problems, working around constraints, and modding whatever they’ve been given. At home, at school, when our kids says something is impossible, make it a priority to prove them wrong. These are skills that can serve them their entire lives. And it starts with us modeling the behaviour, and hacking ourselves.

Maybe the best way to learn thing joyfully and meaningfully is hacking way - the way which kids can learn not only new knowledge, problem solving skills but also prepare them with the right mindset of learning. And moreover, with correct set of topics, problems to solve, hacking can teach about the meaning of their work to others, to environment, to society …

This website is for our “everyday life hacking”, tips, tricks, experiments and our opinions not only in tech, but also in the quest to hack the value of our innerself. We learn from the past, hack it today to disrupt tomorrow.
